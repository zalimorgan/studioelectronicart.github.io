Bio

I am an Emerging artist working on Wadjuk Nyungar Boodja, I recognise the Wadjuk Nyungar people as the rightful custodians of the land that I live, and that their sovereignty over the land was never succeeded. I participate and engage with moments against the racist power structures which our culture is based upon, and work to ensure the my life and practice is expressed in opposition to my colonial past and in support of those that it has silenced and oppressed.

My practice spans sculpture, performance and digital mediums, particularly interested in the translation of artworks between mediums. In 2020 I have been exploring the manifestation of non-Euclidean Geometry, and on the material of the internet within my practice. Sculpture is the foundation of my practice, I am interested in composing formal sculpture's, highlighting edge quality, texture, balance and space. Their exhibition, translation and reinterpretation is the more exploratory and experimental part of my practice, where I work with digital media, print and video to explore a more imaginative reality.

The persistant, repetative, imaginative, child-ish, escapist, immersive and otherworldly aspects to my work are a reflection of my diagnosis with ADHD. I encorage you to reach out to me if you wish to discuss art (mine or yours), science fiction, utilitarianisim, interesting rocks, non-Euclidean Geometry or something bridging any of these two subjects.
